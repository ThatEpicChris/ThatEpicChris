The Assignment
Demonstrate an understanding of Photoshop through the use of cropping, retouching, blending, adjusting, filtering, and stylizing images. It can either be believeable or abstract; however all work must be non-destructive within the editing process.
Specific workflows that should be demonstrated through the works include Image Size/Resolution, Cropping and Retouching, Adjustment Layers, and Basic Selction. Growth from group feedback session should also be incorporated on the second iteration.
The file had to be 10 inches by 8 inches at 300ppi. RGB color mode and a transparent background were required as well. All images used must follow Creative Commons licensing or be original.

The First Iteration
Myths - First Iteration
The first iteration took the idea of exemplifying the beauty of NYC while showing the shadows behind it's shining light. I resized and adjusted the position of the original image among the canvas I had within Photoshop to be positioned like a polaroid photo, utilizing it's iconic 'border' to add to the story of the image of NYC.
I then took a selction of a circular ray on the NYC photo to manipulate the saturation; creating an abstract look among the sky.
I also used the Healing Brush and Spot Healing Brush to remove the Verizon logo on the building in the middle, vents on the right-most building, and windows on the second right-most building.

Feedback
Key feedback elements include enhancing the colorful grafitti at the bottom of the photo, otherwise provide some emphasis on it as it disappears from the photo altogether. Another strong feedback point was to remove the large ladder from the rooftop at the bottom. It's presence only detracted from the overall work. Lastly, the border is nice - however white is too contrasting with the image, other options should be explored.
The Second Iteration
Myths - Second Iteration
In keeping with the NYC theme, I changed the border to match the visual style of the NYC Subway; white line and Helvetica text in all. I chose the words "Beauty Among Chaos" as it best encapsulates the message of how NYC is a very chaotic environment, but simultaneously it can be very beautiful.
Personally, the fact that the image disappeared from below created a sense of immersiveness and blended nicely with the background. As per group feedback, I removed the ladder that detracted from the image via the Healing Brush and Stamp Tool; the result certainly was more pleasing than what was prior. For the skyline, I reduced the saturation to a grayscale to provide more emphasis on select aspects with color; one was the grafitti at the bottom. The colors were certainly exoctic; however I adjusted them with an adjustment layer and selection mask to match those from the skyline. Lastly, I used an adjustment layer and selction mask to provide some emphasis on a muscian playing on a piano, just to the left of the grafitti.

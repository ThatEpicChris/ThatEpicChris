The Assignment
Demonstrate an understanding of Photoshop through composition and use of selection and transformation tools. All work must be non-destructive within the editing process.
Specific workflows that should be demonstrated through the works include Layer Management, Advanced Selection, Masking, and Transformation workflows. From the MYTH assignment, the incorporation of Blends, Effects, and/or Filter routines are to be carried over to this assignment. Growth from group feedback session should also be incorporated on the second iteration.
The file had to be 10 inches by 8 inches at 300ppi. RGB color mode and a transparent background were required as well. All images used must follow Creative Commons licensing or be original.

The First Iteration
Remix - First Iteration
The first iteration tells the story of success, and encourages the viewer to "Start Somewhere". The image collages 6 images together to show how one must start somewhere, often not in the prettiest places, to rise through the ranks to achieve something on the grand stage. This work incorporates workflows from MYTH, including the removal of the TV on the right side of the wall in the 'slum gym'. I used basic transformation and selection tools to position the 'slum gym' and the arena backdrop into place. The backdrop was grayscaled via an adjustment layer to reduce it's presence in the photo; much like how one desires for this final stage, but it's presence in reality is as present in the photo here.
The images in the windows show the various stages it takes to get to the grand stage (backdrop graphic). They are positioned in the windows with a selection mask and a blending of 'multiply' to let the paneling of the windows be present.

The Second Iteration
Myths - Second Iteration
This iteration continues the same basketball theme, the text focusing more on winning the game than rising through the level of sport. I used a selection mask to outline the jumping player and the hoop; and positioned them with the basic transformational tools. The background utilizes adjustment layers to grayscale everything but the videoboard (done with a soft brush to let the 'light of it' shine beyond it's strict border). I used the Dry Brush from the filter gallery to provide some texture to this background layer as well.
